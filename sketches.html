100 SKETCHES OF 100 BUILDINGS
100 Sketches of 100 Buildings is an architectural drawing project that was inspired by the book 100 Buildings. Produced by The Now Institute with a foreword by Thom Mayne, 100 Buildings is a catalogue of the most important buildings of the 20th century. This list was compiled by asking renowned architects and firms to choose the buildings they thought every student of architecture should know. The top 100 were selected based on the frequency of which they were stated.

A designer and aspiring architect, I wanted to study these works past their written history and descriptions, so I drew every single one, studying their forms and plans as I went. An hour to thirty minutes were spent on each sketch, while another hour to thirty minutes were spent doing online research to learn more about the building a little more than the book allowed.
This website acts as an archive to this work, and as an extension of the physical book for those that are interested. The globe below denotes the physical location of each building by pulsing dots. This was a detail that I wish were in the book, and I hope this allows the reader to place themselves physically in respect to these incredible works.
